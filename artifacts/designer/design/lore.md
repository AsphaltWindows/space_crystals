# Lore

## Space Crystals

Space Crystals arrived on Earth via a meteorite impact. After landing, they began gradually self-replicating.

### Properties

Space Crystals have two categories of useful properties:

- **Scientific/Manufacturing**: Valuable for research and industrial applications.
- **Psychoactive**: When groups of sentient creatures consume Space Crystals in proximity to one another, they experience an altered state of consciousness — a sense of euphoria and connectedness with those consuming nearby. Over time, the ego and identity of the heaviest consumer imprints on the others in the group. The stronger the consumption, the stronger the imprinting effect.

Space Crystals are significantly neurotoxic with long-term use.

## Factions

### GDO (Global Defense Organization)

First-world nations with the state capacity to clean up Space Crystals from their territories after the meteorite impact. Their societies are largely unaffected by the psychoactive properties. Their interest in crystals is controlled and institutional — research and manufacturing.

### Syndicate

A collection of criminal, terrorist, and rebel organizations operating across faction boundaries. Some have ideological convictions about the role of Space Crystals for humanity. Others are purely profit-driven, smuggling crystals into cleaned GDO territories through corrupt connections.

### Cults

In less developed regions where state capacity did not allow for crystal cleanup, widespread consumption subverted weak existing institutions. Cult leaders emerge by consuming the most crystals, imprinting their ego and identity on followers in the vicinity. However, the neurotoxicity of heavy consumption leads to deteriorating health and untimely deaths among leaders, making Cult society inherently unstable.

### Colonists

Firms from a distant alien civilization. Their world experienced its own crystal impact long ago, and their society has since evolved and stabilized around the use and consumption of Space Crystals. They are arriving on Earth as corporate operations to harvest the planet's crystals for profit.
